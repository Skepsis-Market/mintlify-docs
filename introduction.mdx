## What Is Skepsis

Skepsis is a **continuous outcome market**. Instead of binary Yes/No positions, you bet on the exact range where an outcome will land. Narrower conviction means better odds.

### The Precision Premium

<Frame caption="Same market move, higher payout for predicting a specific range.">
  <img src="/images/precision-chart-v2.svg" alt="Precision Premium Chart Comparison" />
</Frame>

Bitcoin is at **\$95,000**. You're confident it ends the week between **\$98,000 and \$99,000**.

Binary bet vs. Skepsis bet:

| | Binary Market (Polymarket) | Skepsis |
|---|---|---|
| **The Question** | "Will BTC > \$97k?" | "Price of BTC?" |
| **Your Bet** | YES | Range: \$98k - \$99k |
| **The Crowd** | 50/50 split | Spread across \$90k-\$110k |
| **The Odds** | ~2.0x (Double your money) | **~10.0x (Ten times your money)** |
| **The Result** | BTC hits \$98.5k. You win. | BTC hits \$98.5k. You win. |

On the binary market, "it stops at \$99k" is priced the same as "it goes up." Skepsis pays for precision.

---

## Range Betting vs. Binary

<Frame caption="Binary markets spread capital across a wide outcome space. Range markets concentrate it.">
  <img src="/images/outcome-ui.svg" alt="Outcome Resolution Comparison" />
</Frame>

*   **Binary markets** cover broad targets ("Up", "Down", "Yes", "No"). Low miss rate, but low reward for accuracy.
*   **Range markets** are for traders with a specific thesis. Narrower range = higher payout when correct.
