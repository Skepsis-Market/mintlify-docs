*A groom hedges his wedding weather. A planner bets on local knowledge. The cold front wins.*

---

## The Market

Miguel is planning a beach wedding in Miami next Saturday. He finds a Skepsis market:

> **"What will the high temperature in Miami be on Saturday, December 14th?"**
>
> **Range:** 65°F - 95°F
> **Resolution:** Saturday 6:00 PM local time
> **Liquidity:** $5,000 USDC

```
65°F - 70°F:  2%   ░
70°F - 75°F:  8%   ░░░
75°F - 80°F:  25%  ░░░░░░░░░░
80°F - 85°F:  35%  ░░░░░░░░░░░░░░  ← Most likely
85°F - 90°F:  22%  ░░░░░░░░░
90°F - 95°F:  8%   ░░░
```

Miguel wants 78-82°F. Too cold is uncomfortable. Too hot means sweaty photos and wilting flowers.

---

## The Bets

**Miguel (the groom) — hedging, not speculating.** If it's 85°F+, he'll spend ~$200 on extra fans and ice. So he buys the "too hot" zone as insurance.

- 85°F - 95°F / $50 USDC / 3.3x / $165 potential payout
- If it's hot: winnings cover the extra costs. If it's perfect: he "loses" $50 but has a great wedding.

**Sophia (the bride)** — forecast says 79°F with high confidence. She bets on perfect weather.

- 76°F - 82°F / $75 USDC / 2.5x / $187.50 potential payout

**James (wedding planner, 200+ Miami weddings)** — December cold fronts happen more than tourists think. The 65-75°F range is underpriced.

- 70°F - 76°F / $100 USDC / 6x / $600 potential payout

**Maria (40-year Miami resident)** — convinced it'll be scorching. The 90°F+ range at 8% looks like a steal.

- 90°F - 95°F / $30 USDC / 12.5x / $375 potential payout

---

## The Week

**Monday:** Forecast says 81°F. Sophia feels good.

**Wednesday:** Updated forecast: 78°F. James raises an eyebrow.

**Friday evening:** Cold front warning. Temperature might drop to low 70s.

**Saturday morning:** Cool and breezy. Guests comment on the "perfect weather."

**Saturday 6:00 PM — Resolution:** Official high was **74°F**.

---

## Results

| Player | Range | Result |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Miguel | 85°F - 95°F | LOSE — wasn't hot, but the wedding was perfect |
| Sophia | 76°F - 82°F | LOSE — 2°F too cold |
| James | 70°F - 76°F | **WIN — $600** |
| Maria | 90°F - 95°F | LOSE |

James's experience spotting underpriced cold fronts paid off. Miguel lost $50 but got perfect wedding weather — that's the hedge working as intended.

Sophia was 2°F away. A wider range (74-84°F at ~1.8x) would have captured it. Narrower range = better odds but lower hit rate. This is always the tradeoff.

---

## Prediction markets as insurance

Miguel wasn't gambling. He was buying a $50 hedge against a specific bad outcome, with instant payout if it hit. Traditional event insurance means filing claims and waiting weeks. A prediction market position pays out on-chain the moment the market resolves.
