*Three traders. One BTC market. Nobody wins.*

---

## The Market

It's 2:00 PM on a Tuesday. Bitcoin is at $96,450. Skepsis has a 1-hour market:

> **"Where will BTC/USD be at 3:00 PM UTC?"**
>
> **Range:** $94,000 - $100,000
> **Resolution:** 1 hour
> **Liquidity:** $10,000 USDC

Current distribution:

```
$94,000 - $95,000:  3%   ░░
$95,000 - $96,000:  12%  ░░░░░
$96,000 - $97,000:  35%  ░░░░░░░░░░░░░░  ← Current price zone
$97,000 - $98,000:  28%  ░░░░░░░░░░░
$98,000 - $99,000:  15%  ░░░░░░
$99,000 - $100,000: 7%   ░░░
```

---

## Sarah: The Range Player

Watches BTC all morning. Thinks it stays flat, maybe drifts up slightly.

**Bet:** $96,500 - $97,500 / $100 USDC / 3.2x / $320 potential payout

She's in the popular zone — lower odds but high confidence.

---

## Marcus: The Contrarian

Sees a headline about potential Fed comments. Expects a dip.

**Bet:** $95,000 - $96,000 / $150 USDC / 8.3x / $1,245 potential payout

Betting against the crowd. Low probability, better odds.

---

## Charlie: The Sniper

Day trader watching order books. Unusual buy pressure building.

**Bet:** $98,000 - $98,500 (narrow) / $50 USDC / 12x / $600 potential payout

High-risk, high-reward on a tight range.

---

## The Hour

**2:15 PM:** BTC dips to $96,200. Marcus feels validated.

**2:30 PM:** BTC recovers to $96,600. Sarah's looking good.

**2:45 PM:** Buying pressure. BTC jumps to $97,100.

**2:55 PM:** Another push. $97,400... $97,600... $97,800...

**3:00 PM — Resolution:** BTC closes at **$97,650**

---

## Results

| Trader | Range | Result | Notes |
|--------|-------|--------|-------|
| Sarah | $96,500 - $97,500 | LOSE | $150 away from winning |
| Marcus | $95,000 - $96,000 | LOSE | Price never dropped that far |
| Charlie | $98,000 - $98,500 | LOSE | Close, didn't reach |

Nobody won. The outcome landed in $97,500 - $98,000 — a range at ~20% probability that none of them bet on.

---

## What went wrong

**Sarah was $150 off.** In a binary market ("BTC stays under $98K"), she'd have been right. Continuous markets reward precision, and she was almost precise enough. If she'd split $100 into $70 on $96,500-$97,500 and $30 on $97,500-$98,500 (5x), the second leg would have paid $150 — netting +$50 overall.

**Marcus had the wrong direction.** Contrarian bets pay well when they hit, but the macro catalyst didn't materialize in time.

**Charlie was close but narrow.** A $97,500-$99,000 range at lower odds would have captured it.

The crowd priced $96K-$98K at ~78% combined, and the outcome landed right there — just not where any of these three specified.
